Recurrent sequence models are limited by sequential computation, which prevents parallelization and makes it difficult to learn dependencies between distant positions 1 . To address this, the Transformer is proposed as the first transduction model relying entirely on self-attention to compute representations without using recurrent or convolutional layers 2 . The methodology uses a stacked encoder-decoder architecture featuring multi-head attention and point-wise feed-forward networks 3 . Multi-head attention allows the model to jointly attend to information from different representation subspaces at different positions 4 . In evaluations, the Transformer achieved a state-of-the-art 28.4 BLEU on English-to-German and 41.8 BLEU on English-to-French translation tasks 5 . These results were obtained in significantly less training time than prior models, with the base model requiring only twelve hours on eight GPUs 6
